History of Shelties

All about Shelties

Shelties originated in the Shetland Islands of Scotland, a remote and rugged region known for its harsh climate and small livestock. Much like the small Shetland pony, the Sheltie was bred to be a miniature herding dog, capable of working in challenging conditions.

Different types of shelties

Sable
Ranges from golden to mahogany. Can be sable and white, shaded sable, or mahogany sable. Most common Sheltie color.
Tricolor
Black base coat with white and tan markings. A classic and popular coloration.
Blue Merle
Mottled gray, black, and white coat with tan points. Often has blue or partially blue eyes.
Bi-Black
Black and white only, with no tan markings.
